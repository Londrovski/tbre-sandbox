Make it actually happen — get the parts ordered in time and on budget, built, checked, and tested on the physical car, not just designed on a screen.

## Owns
- Ordering the parts
- Building the parts
- Testing the parts

## Delivers
- Parts ordered on time and on budget, with the cost info flowing to Finance
- Parts checked for validity early, before they go on the car
- The system tested on the physical car
- Pressure across the whole team to test on the physical car as soon as it can move

## What's involved
A design only counts once it's on the car and working. This responsibility owns the path from CAD to a tested part: ordering early enough that there's time to build and test, giving the Finance Lead the information they need to place orders on time and on budget, checking parts as they arrive, and pushing for test time so the actuator gets onto the real car at the earliest point it can do something. It's the delivery-discipline half of the role — the part that keeps the project landing rather than drifting.

## This year (context, not structure)
- **Finance Lead** — give them the supplier, cost and lead-time information early so orders are placed in time (HPC and the motor supplier are already known routes).
- **Order early** so there's a build-and-test window, not a scramble.
- **Push for test time** and get a basic, motion-capable actuator on the car for the **postseason testing window (early July)** — even "moves itself in a circle" counts. The polished design follows.
- **James** can be boots-on-the-ground in Bath over summer for assembly, fitting and fault-finding.

## Open questions
- Component lead times, and the order-by dates that protect the test window.
- When mechanical test time on the car can be booked.
