Own the EBS's relationships across the team — so it integrates with the car's brakes, triggers correctly off the safety system, and mounts and packages cleanly.

## Owns
- A defined, agreed space in the general assembly (and the mounting that holds it)
- The technical relationships that keep the EBS integrated — manual brakes, vehicle dynamics, and the shutdown / trigger side

## Delivers
- An EBS fully integrated into the car's brake system and the AI safety chain
- Agreed brake bias and hydraulic integration with VD, and a clean trigger interface with the VCU

## What's involved
The EBS doesn't stand alone — it taps into the existing hydraulic brakes, is triggered by the electrical safety system, and has to be packaged and mounted within tight rules. This responsibility is the coordination: agree the brake balance and hydraulic tie-in with Vehicle Dynamics, agree the trigger and monitoring interface with the VCU/electrical side, and hold a defined, compliant space in the general assembly with the chassis team.

## This year (context, not structure)
- **Vehicle Dynamics** — the brake bias and deceleration target, and tying into the existing manual hydraulic circuits via shuttle valves (no effect on manual braking).
- **VCU Lead (Shinn)** — the shutdown circuit / ASMS chain that de-energises the solenoid to trigger braking, plus pressure-sensor monitoring for the pre-run check (sensors monitor only; they never command the brake).
- **Chassis team** — mounting the system behind the firewall (which doubles as the gas-system shield), inside the rollover envelope, with the canister oriented away from the driver and the two external brake-release valves placed to rule.

## Open questions
- Placement of the two manual deactivation valves (the "in proximity" rule) on the actual car.
- How the packaging and mounting carry over to next year's car.
